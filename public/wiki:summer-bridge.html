Summer Bridge

EOP Academic Counselors and Peer Academic Counselors provide services to Summer Bridge students including required appointments, drop-in consultations, and occasional workshops. For additional information about Summer Bridge, please visit the Summer Bridge Home Page .

GENERAL OVERVIEW

Depending on Summer Bridge size and demographics, EOP counselors will offer 1 to 2 prescheduled academic counseling appointments with EOP Summer Bridge students. Students are able to schedule follow-up appointments or attend drop-in beyond the prescheduled appointment(s).

Prior to the start of SB, EOP Academic Counselors:

  • Begin meeting periodically in March/April to start thinking about services to be offered in SB.
  • Meet biweekly in May
  • Meet weekly in June
  • Support in the training of Peer Academic Counselors
  • Are assigned a given amount of students for their caseload. In the past number have ranged from 40-55.

- Counselors working with specialized populations are assigned students from those populations (i.e. USP, BHS, etc.)

During the 6 weeks of SB EOP Academic Counselors:

  • Attend weekly EOP/USP staff meetings to discuss trends, rising issues, updates from the SB office, and to collaborate on necessary action plans
  • Are in direct contact with campus partners, such as: Summer Bridge Office, Financial Aid & Scholarships Office, Residency Office, Summer Bridge instructors, college advisors, and others during this 6 week program to support the holistic transition of each student.

After SB ends, EOP Academic Counselors:

  • Follow-up on any outstanding issues with particular students
  • Continue to be available for students to meet with
  • Do grade checks at the end of each semester for their caseload
