EOP Application (to confirm EOP status)

Description
Students are notified with a confirmation of their EOP status via MyBerkeleyApp or with a direct email from our office once they are admitted. If a student was not identified as EOP but feels they meet the criteria, they may apply directly to our office to verify EOP status via the online EOP Application . Individuals who meet ANY ONE of the three criteria listed below are considered EOP:

1) Low Income (Pell Grant or Dream Act Scholarship recipients as verified by Financial Aid)

2) First Generation College Students (Parent[s] have not graduated from a four-year college of university in the United States)

3) Historically Underrepresented Students (African American, Chicano/Latino, Native American)

**Please note that non-immigrant international students studying at Berkeley on student visas are not considered EOP.

The Process
Student staff must first check the student into Quincy before EOP verification process begins. Next, they will contact a counselor to verify the students EOP status. The counselor can use the following systems to verify:

MyFinAid : Students who receive the Pell Grant or Dream Act Scholarship qualify as low-income and EOP

Myberkeley App : Students coded as L4 by the Office of Undergraduate Admissions qualify as low-income and first-generation

DB2 : Students are coded by ethnic backgrounds in this system. By UC definition, historically underrepresented communities include African-American/Black, Chicano/Latino & Native American.

Checking Quincy and/or speaking to counselor should serve to verify EOP status in most cases. In those instances when status cannot be immediately verified and a student is not in Quincy and a counselor is not available, students can apply by using the online EOP application . It is the responsibility of the day's primary drop-in counselor to check the EOP application responses GoogleDoc and process each open application. Upon processing the application, staff should input the student information into Quincy, create an "admin contact" in Quincy that describes that an EOP Application has been processed, and notify the student via email. Only low-income verified students are eligible for EOP financial resources such as the discretionary grant, Berkeley Art Studio Scholarship, and grad prep course fee reductions.

EOP Status in Quincy

The first 5 EOP status categories in a students Quincy profile are not up to date with the current EOP status definition. They are included solely for tagging purposes for students prior to the most recent EOP status. Below are the 4 current categories that counselors will use in classifying students EOP status.

1) EOP by application

  • Student does not receive Pell Grant or Dream Act
  • Student is first-generation OR identifies with a historically underrepresented population. Berkeley defines underrepresented as African American, Chicano/Latino, Native American but counselors have the discretion to approve as UREM those students that indicate South Asian, Vietnamese, Pacific Islander, or "other" category in the online application.

2) EOP/LI by Admission

  • L4 code in MyBerkeleyApp Student is first-generation, low-income and California resident

3) EOP/LI by Application

  • Student completed EOP application form and is a Pell or Dream recipient
  • Students EOP low-income status was verified by a counselor using one of the above systems. The term Application is only used for tagging purposes.

4) Not EOP

  • Student does not meet any of the above criteria.
