Peer Academic Counseling Program

EOP Peer Academic Counselors (or PACs) offer advising for undergraduate students using a holistic and multicultural counseling model. PACs undergo an extensive training process throughout the semester that features many campus departments and EOP partners. Students can seek assistance from PACs for a range of reasons including, but not limited to academic advising, general financial aid advising, major exploration and transitional support.

The Process
Before applying to the PAC program, students must first apply and participate in the iPAC internship program . Upon meeting performance standards, iPACs are invited to apply to be a PAC and possibly interview for the position.

During the academic year, the PAC program consists of weekly staff meetings (1 hour), trainings (2 hours), office hours and project hours. PACs participate in many collaborative events with departments on campus during the academic year. During the Summer, PACs work closely with an EOP/USP counselor and their Summer Bridge cohort. The PACs Summer Bridge schedule consist of 3 weeks of intense trainings, welcome weekend staffing, weekly meetings with assigned counselor, cohort midterm check-ins, facilitating weekly seminars, office hours, and weekly staff meetings.

There are three tiers for PACs:

  • Entry-level
  • Mid-level
  • PAC Coordinator

The PAC Supervisor is responsible for the following:

  • Meeting weekly with the PAC Coordinator
  • Reviewing and approving all PAC timesheets
  • Assisting with the planning of weekly meetings
  • Individual semesterly check-ins with PACs to enhance their professional development
  • Attending bPAN (Berkeley Peer Advisor Network) events/trainings
