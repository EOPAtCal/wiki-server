USP Emergency Grant

Emergency Grants provide emergency funding for extenuating circumstances to undocumented students for up to $500. The housing emergency grant is intended for extenuating housing related costs whereas the general emergency grant is intended for extenuating food security, medical, dental, computer, and other unforeseen emergencies. Emergency grants do not have to be paid back and students apply through an on-line application. Students are awarded based on need and on a first come first serve basis. Students can only receive each emergency grant one time per academic year.

The Process
The google form application is created as early as the 3rd week of the semester and is closed when funds exhaust. A new google form application is made for each semester. This is put on website and sent out to students. After students apply, applications, additional documentation, and financial aid packages are reviewed. Upon completion, Stipend Award forms are completed, signed, and approved. The award is then put into DSAS to pay out to students CARs account.

Language on Application:

The Undocumented Student Program at UC Berkeley is now taking applications for the fall 2014 General & Housing Emergency Grants. Please read the following in detail prior to completing the application:

1. These grants are for emergencies only. They are not intended to supplement financial aid or unmet need, but for extenuating circumstances.
2. Each grant is for up to $500
3. The General grant is for: medical services, dental or vision services, graduate school related costs, and other emergency expenses as determined by the reviewer. It is not for tuition, fees, books, credit card bills, etc.
4. The Housing grant is only for extenuating housing expenses such as transitional housing, deposits, etc.

Note: Your financial aid package and budgeting will be evaluated in the review of your application

To be eligible, you must meet the following:
a) a currently enrolled undocumented student at UCB
b) low income as defined by financial aid guidelines and NOT be eligible for Federal financial aid
c) not have outstanding balance to the university on CARS bill
d) present valid documentation of need and/or proof of purchase (Electronic submission to undocu@berkeley.edu )

** GRANTS WILL ONLY BE REVIEWED AND APPROVED ONCE A WEEK.

AFTER APPROVAL, IT WILL TAKE 12 BUSINESS DAYS TO RECEIVE FUNDS. PLEASE KEEP THIS IN MIND AS YOU APPLY.
