Transfer Student Center

MISSION:

The center provides services to assist transfer students with navigating the academic and cultural landscape of the research university. We serve students who transfer to UC Berkeley from other institutions, those who are returning to their education after an interruption, and who are combining school with parenting. Our programs and services focus on supporting a successful transition, building connection and community, and assisting students as they pursue their academic and career goals. The center also plays a key role in campus outreach and recruitment through yield events and participation in programs involving California Community Colleges.

The Transfer Student Center is a unit within The Centers for Educational Equity and Excellence, which strives to empower non-traditional, underserved, and underrepresented students with the support, expertise, and advocacy required to achieve at Berkeley and beyond.

Overview Of Services Provided:

ENGAGEMENT AND CONNECTION:

  • Community Center

Located in 100 Cesar Chavez Student Center, the center provides a welcoming place where new and continuing transfer students can find information, advice, and support as they transition in and move through the university. The center provides an information desk staffed by trained and experienced peers, study tables, couches, computers, print station, resource information, a microwave, coffee and tea.

  • Service Learning Internships

Internships are developed each semester and focus on addressing community needs, fostering leadership, and public service. Students can earn academic credit while they take part in serving the needs of a dynamic student community.

  • Community Building Events

Community events are designed to assist with building connection and community for new and continuing tranfer students at Cal. The events are organized twice a semester and focus on building social networks and introducing students to the resources and programs available to them at the transfer student center. Food and refreshments are provided.

  • Video Team

Student Staff work together to create videos for the Transfer Center that are meant to spread information. This information can range from facts about the center, to student voices that are intended to make their peers feel more comfortable and connected. These videos are primarily hosted on YouTube, then shared to our website. Our Director, Lorena Valdez, applied for a Tech Grant that funds this project.

CAMPUS AND COMMUNITY PARTNERSHIP :

  • The center provides a community space and a welcoming environment for students. The front desk is staffed by transfer student peer advocates who can assist in answering questions and connecting you to campus resources. The Center offers study spaces, comfortable couches, and a small kitchen area offering a microwave, toaster, coffee, and tea.

TRANSITION AND NAVIGATION:

  • Advising

Transitioning to a 4-year research university from another college or university can be challenging. Our staff practices a holistic and solution-focused approach that helps you consider your options and explore the possibilities UC Berkeley provides.

  • Mentoring

As a first semester transfer student you will have the opportunity to take part in the Major Insights Mentoring Program and talk with a fellow transfer student who has had a year of experience in your major. Learn about academic expectations, working with professors and graduate student instructors, and strategies to succeed in your major.

  • Education 198 Transition Course

Education 198 is a course offering designed to help facilitate your transition to Cal by improving your knowledge of the research university and its resources, helping you to identify key academic skills and strategies for upper division coursework and helps new transfer students build connections and community.

  • Academic Workshops

This workshop series introduces transfer students to various campus resources, opportunities, and academic strategies. The workshops are facilitated by campus representatives and tailored to a transfer student audience. The workshops are a great opportunity for transfer students to learn about campus programs, resources, and ways to engage with the research university.
